# Personal finance (2023-2024) using  MS Excel
## Background | Overview
This dataset is a multi-person personal finance tracker designed to monitor the financial expenses and income patterns of four individuals, and to compare financial behavior across the personal identifiers 00JS, 00Ed, 00MM, and 00Jd, spanning the years 2023–2024.
This project seeks to uncover critical insights into the spending behavior of each individual, with a focus on the following key areas:
1.	Overall Expenditure -An evaluation of the total sum of expenses incurred over the period.
2.	Total Income - An analysis of the total income of each individual to determine the level of expenditure that could be sustained.
3.	Total Expenditure & Income -A thorough analysis of how individual income levels influence spending habits.
4.	Expenditure of All Persons in 2024 -A dedicated review of spending patterns across all four individuals within the year 2024.

# Executive Summary
Following an analytical comparison of financial behavior across the personal identifiers 00JS, 00Ed, 00MM, and 00Jd for the period 2023-2024, the highest financial expenditure was recorded by 00MM, with significant KPIs consistently reflecting elevated financial expenses relative to the other individuals. The dataset classifies every transaction as either a "Want" or a "Need." Key discretionary categories draining finances include Food Delivery, Restaurants, Streaming Services, Hotel Stays, Flight Tickets, Car Rentals, Clothing, Online Shopping, GPT Premium, and Membership Fees. all of which reoccur across both years for all four individuals.

# Recommendations
1.	Monthly Net Worth Tracking, each individual should track their personal net worth on a monthly basis. Observing consistent growth serves as a powerful motivator for sustained financial discipline. Additionally, automate a 20% savings deduction on every income payment across all four individuals.
2.	Lifestyle Inflation Guard, as income grows (as evidenced in the 2023 vs. 2024 comparison), avoid proportionally increasing lifestyle spending. Cap lifestyle expense growth at 50% of the income growth rate, and launch monthly accountability check-ins among the four individuals beginning immediately.
3.	Financial Accountability Partner, pair individuals together for monthly check-ins on savings goals, fostering a culture of shared financial accountability within the group. Audit and cancel all low-value subscriptions within the next 30 days.
